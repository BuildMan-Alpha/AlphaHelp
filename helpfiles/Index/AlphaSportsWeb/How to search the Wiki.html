Page Title Search

Page Title Search

Exact match

Page title searches usually give fewer results than full-text searches. The auto-suggest list from this box can put you on the right page in two clicks. Note: The auto-suggest list takes a few seconds to appear. If you would rather see the search results on a separate page, turn Exact Match off.

Full-text Search

Full-text Search




Full-text searches can include Boolean operations in Advanced mode. Add a plus sign (e.g. +xbasic +sql) before a term to indicate that the term must appear in the results. Click on the ? for help, especially if the syntax you're using for Advanced search doesn't work the way you expect. Note: searches for very short words, such as "xml", work better with Advanced Search turned off.

Google Search

Google Search

 
wiki.alphasoftware.com
WWW

Google Search can be useful, but is sometimes out of date. Google Search often finds more pages than the site's own Full-text search, because Google finds search terms that are part of longer strings, and Full-text search looks only for separate words.
